Instructions

In a large bowl, combine the minced lamb, finely chopped onion, ginger-garlic paste, green chilies, fresh coriander leaves, red chili powder, coriander powder, cumin powder, garam masala, roasted gram flour (or breadcrumbs, if using), and salt.
Mix all the ingredients thoroughly with your hands. Ensure everything is well combined.
Divide the mixture into small, equal-sized balls (kofta). Wet your hands with a little water to prevent the mixture from sticking.
For Frying: Heat oil in a deep pan or kadai over medium heat. Gently slide the kofta into the hot oil, ensuring not to overcrowd the pan. Fry the kofta on medium heat until they are golden brown and cooked through, turning them occasionally for even cooking. This should take about 8-10 minutes.
Remove the fried kofta from the oil using a slotted spoon and place them on a paper towel-lined plate to drain excess oil.
For Grilling: Preheat your grill to medium heat. Lightly oil the grill grates. Place the kofta on the grill and cook for about 10-12 minutes, turning occasionally, until they are cooked through and have slight char marks.
Once cooked, remove the lamb kofta from the oil or grill.
Serve hot as an appetizer with mint chutney or yogurt dip. Alternatively, add them to your favorite curry gravy.
