Vegetable Korma

A creamy and flavorful dish with mixed vegetables cooked in a rich and aromatic gravy.

Instructions

Wash and chop all the vegetables into uniform cubes. Soak cashew nuts and poppy seeds in warm water for at least 15-20 minutes. Grind them together into a smooth paste.
Heat oil or ghee in a heavy-bottomed pot or pan over medium heat. Add the finely chopped onions and sauté until they turn light golden brown.
Add the ginger-garlic paste and sauté for about a minute until fragrant.
Add the green cardamom pods, cloves, and cinnamon stick to the pot. Sauté for a minute, releasing their aroma.
Add the cubed mixed vegetables to the pot. Sauté for 2-3 minutes.
Pour in the cashew-poppy seed paste and coriander powder. Mix well and cook for 2-3 minutes.
Add the yogurt and turmeric powder. Mix well and cook for another 2-3 minutes until the oil starts to separate from the sides.
Add about 1/2 cup of water (or more, depending on the desired consistency). Bring the mixture to a simmer, then reduce the heat to low, cover the pot, and cook for 10-15 minutes, or until the vegetables are cooked through and tender.
Stir in the garam masala and salt to taste. Cook for another 2-3 minutes.
Garnish with freshly chopped coriander leaves.
Serve the vegetable korma hot with naan, roti, paratha, rice, or any Indian bread of your choice.
