Instructions

Boil Spices: In a saucepan, combine water, crushed ginger, cardamom pods, and cinnamon stick. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer for 3-4 minutes to extract flavors. The water should turn slightly golden.
Add Tea: Add tea leaves (or tea bags) and simmer for 1 minute. For stronger chai, simmer 30 seconds longer. Don't overboil or the tea will become bitter.
Add Milk: Pour in milk and bring to a gentle boil. Watch carefully as milk can boil over quickly. Reduce heat and simmer for 2-3 minutes until the color changes to creamy brown.
Sweeten: Add sugar and stir well. Traditional chai is quite sweet, but adjust to your preference. For healthier option, use jaggery or honey instead.
Strain & Serve: Strain into cups through a fine mesh strainer. For authentic "chaiwallah style", pour from height to create froth. Serve immediately with biscuits or snacks. For iced version, let cool then pour over ice.
