Instructions

Soaking: Rinse the green gram (moong dal) thoroughly under cold water. Soak it in enough water for at least 4-6 hours, or preferably overnight.
Grinding: Drain all the water from the soaked green gram. Transfer it to a blender along with the green chilies, ginger, and a little water (about 1/4 cup).
Blending: Blend to a smooth, dosa-like batter consistency. Add more water if needed, but ensure the batter is not too thin.
Seasoning: Add salt to the batter and mix well. If using, you can also add a tablespoon of finely chopped onion to the batter at this stage.
Cooking: Heat a flat griddle or non-stick pan over medium heat. Once hot, lightly grease it with oil.
Spreading: Pour a ladleful of batter onto the hot griddle and spread it in a circular motion to form a thin crepe.
Cooking Sides: Drizzle some oil around the edges and on the pesarattu. Cook on medium heat until the bottom turns golden brown and crispy (about 2-3 minutes).
Flipping (Optional): You can either fold the pesarattu in half or flip it gently and cook for another minute until the other side is lightly golden.
Serving: Serve hot pesarattu with coconut chutney, tomato chutney, or ginger chutney. Garnish with fresh cilantro if desired.
