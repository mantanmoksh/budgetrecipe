Lamb Korma

A mild and creamy Mughlai dish with tender lamb cooked in a fragrant yogurt-based gravy with nuts and spices.

Instructions

Heat oil or ghee in a heavy-bottomed pot over medium heat. Add green cardamom pods, cloves, and cinnamon stick. Sauté for a few seconds until fragrant.
Add finely chopped onions and sauté until light golden brown.
Stir in ginger-garlic paste and sauté for another minute until the raw smell disappears.
Add the lamb pieces and sauté on medium-high heat until they are browned on all sides.
Lower the heat and add turmeric powder, coriander powder, and cumin powder. Sauté for a minute, stirring constantly.
Whisk the yogurt until smooth and add it to the pot. Stir continuously until the yogurt is well combined with the spices and the mixture starts to simmer.
Add the ground cashew nuts and almond slivers. Mix well.
Add salt to taste and water or stock as needed to create the desired gravy consistency.
Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat to low, cover the pot, and let it simmer for 1 to 1.5 hours, or until the lamb is tender. Stir occasionally to prevent sticking. If using a pressure cooker, cook for about 6-8 whistles on low heat.
Once the lamb is cooked and tender, stir in the garam masala and kewra water (if using). Simmer for another 5 minutes.
Garnish with fresh coriander leaves.
Serve hot with rice, naan, or roti.
