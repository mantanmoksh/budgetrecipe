Instructions

Marinate the chicken pieces with ginger-garlic paste, turmeric powder, red chili powder (if using), and salt for at least 15-20 minutes.
Heat oil in a pan and saut√© the marinated chicken until it's cooked through and slightly browned. Add garam masala and chopped coriander leaves. Squeeze some lemon juice.
In the same pan, or a separate flat griddle, lightly cook the rotis until they are soft and slightly warm. If using egg wash, spread it thinly over one side of the roti and cook until set.
Place the cooked chicken filling in the center of each roti.
Top with thinly sliced onions and bell peppers. You can also add other toppings like chaat masala or sauces (mint chutney, tomato ketchup, etc.).
Roll up the roti tightly, securing the filling inside. You can use butter paper or foil to wrap the bottom half of the roll for easier handling.
Serve the kathi rolls immediately and enjoy!

Optional Vegetarian Filling: You can replace the chicken with paneer (Indian cheese) or mixed vegetables like potatoes, cauliflower, and peas cooked with similar spices.
