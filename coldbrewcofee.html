Instructions

Combine: In a large jar or pitcher, mix coarsely ground coffee with cold water. Stir gently to ensure all grounds are wet. The coarse grind is essential - fine grounds will make the coffee muddy.
Steep: Cover and let sit at room temperature for 12-24 hours. For stronger flavor, go for the full 24 hours. If you prefer less caffeine, steep in the refrigerator for 18-24 hours (cold slows extraction).
Strain: Line a fine-mesh strainer with cheesecloth or use a coffee filter. Strain the coffee into a clean container. This may take several minutes - don't rush it or you'll get sediment.
Concentrate: What you have now is cold brew concentrate. It can be stored in the refrigerator for up to 2 weeks. The flavor actually improves after 24 hours.
Serve: Dilute concentrate with equal parts water or milk over ice. Adjust to your taste - some prefer it stronger (1:1 ratio) or weaker (1:2 ratio). Add sweeteners if desired. Can also be heated for hot coffee!
