Instructions

In a large bowl, combine the whole wheat flour, chopped methi leaves, ginger-green chili paste, turmeric powder, red chili powder (if using), cumin powder, yogurt, oil, and salt.
Mix all the ingredients well. Gradually add water and knead to form asmooth and soft dough.
Cover the dough and let it rest for at least 15-20 minutes.
Divide the dough into small, equal-sized balls (approximately 4 for this recipe).
Take one dough ball and roll it out into a thin circular flatbread (thepla) using a rolling pin and a little dry flour if needed. It should be about 6-7 inches in diameter.
Heat a flat griddle or tawa over medium heat. Place the rolled thepla on the hot tawa.
Cook for a few seconds on one side until small bubbles appear. Flip it over and cook the other side similarly.
Apply a little oil or ghee on both sides of the thepla and cook until golden brown spots appear and the thepla is cooked through and slightly crisp. Press gently with a spatula to ensure even cooking.
Repeat the process with the remaining dough balls.
Serve the methi theplas hot with yogurt, pickle, or any sabzi (vegetable dish) of your choice. They are also great for breakfast, snacks, or as a travel food.
