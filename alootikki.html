Instructions

In a small bowl, combine the mashed potato, finely chopped onion (if using), finely chopped green chili (if using), grated ginger, red chili powder, coriander powder, garam masala, chopped coriander leaves, breadcrumbs or rice flour, and salt.
Mix all the ingredients well until they are evenly combined.
Divide the potato mixture into one portion.
Shape the portion into a round or slightly flattened patty (tikki).
Heat a little oil in a flat pan or skillet over medium heat.
Place the aloo tikki on the hot pan.
Cook the tikki for 3-4 minutes on each side, or until it turns golden brown and crisp.
Once cooked, remove the aloo tikki from the pan and place it on a paper towel-lined plate to absorb any excess oil.
Serve the hot aloo tikki immediately. It can be enjoyed as a snack with ketchup or chutney, or as a base for aloo tikki chaat with yogurt, tamarind chutney, and other toppings.
