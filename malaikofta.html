Instructions

For the Koftas: In a bowl, mix grated paneer, mashed potato, all-purpose flour, cardamom powder, and salt. Add chopped raisins and cashews if using.
Knead into a smooth dough. Divide the dough into small portions and shape them into smooth balls (koftas).
Heat oil in a deep pan over medium heat. Gently slide in the koftas and deep-fry them until golden brown. Drain on paper towels and set aside.
For the Gravy: Heat oil or butter in a pan. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter.
Add chopped onions and saut√© until golden brown. Then add chopped ginger and garlic, and saut√© for another minute.
Add chopped tomatoes and cook until they soften and the oil starts to separate.
Let the mixture cool slightly, then blend it into a smooth puree using a hand blender or in a regular blender.
Pour the puree back into the pan. Add turmeric powder and coriander powder. Cook for a minute.
Add milk or cream and garam masala. Simmer for 5-7 minutes, stirring occasionally. Add salt to taste.
Gently add the fried koftas to the gravy just before serving. Simmer for a couple of minutes.
Garnish with fresh coriander leaves and serve hot with naan, roti, or rice.
