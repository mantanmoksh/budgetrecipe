Instructions

Wash the basmati rice thoroughly under running water until the water runs clear. Soak the rice in water for at least 15-20 minutes. Drain well.
Heat oil or ghee in a heavy-bottomed pot or pan over medium heat.
Add cumin seeds to the hot oil or ghee. Let them splutter for a few seconds until they turn light brown and release their aroma. Be careful not to burn them.
Add the drained rice to the pot and saut√© for a minute, stirring gently to coat the grains with the oil/ghee and cumin.
Pour in the water and add salt to taste. Bring the water to a boil.
Once the water is boiling, reduce the heat to low, cover the pot tightly with a lid, and let it simmer for 12-15 minutes, or until all the water is absorbed and the rice is cooked through. Avoid lifting the lid during this time to allow the steam to cook the rice properly.
Once cooked, turn off the heat and let the rice rest, covered, for 5 minutes. This allows the steam to settle and the grains to become fluffy.
Gently fluff the jeera rice with a fork before serving. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves (optional). Serve hot as a side dish with your favorite Indian curries or dals.
