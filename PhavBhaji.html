Pav Bhaji

A popular Indian street food consisting of a thick vegetable curry (bhaji) served with soft bread rolls (pav).

Instructions

Prepare the Vegetables: Boil potatoes, cauliflower, carrots, and green peas until they are soft and easily mashable. You can pressure cook them or boil them in a pot with water. Drain the water and mash the vegetables well.
Sauté Onions and Tomatoes: Heat oil or butter in a large pan or kadhai over medium heat. Add finely chopped onions and sauté until golden brown.
Add ginger-garlic paste and sauté for about a minute until fragrant.
Stir in finely chopped tomatoes and cook until they soften and the oil starts to separate.
Add Spices: Add turmeric powder and red chili powder. Sauté for a few seconds.
Add pav bhaji masala and salt to taste. Mix well and cook for another minute.
Mash and Combine: Add the mashed vegetables to the pan. Mix everything well and mash further with a potato masher to get a smooth and thick consistency.
Add some water if the bhaji is too thick. Simmer for 5-7 minutes, stirring occasionally. You can add more butter at this stage for a richer flavor.
Toast the Pav: While the bhaji is simmering, slice the pav horizontally, keeping one side attached. Heat a flat pan or griddle over medium heat. Apply some butter on both sides of the pav and toast until light golden brown and slightly crispy.
Serve: Serve the hot bhaji garnished with finely chopped onion and a dollop of butter. Serve the toasted pav alongside with lemon wedges. Enjoy immediately!
