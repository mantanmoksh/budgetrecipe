Instructions

Drain the soaked black chickpeas and rinse them well.
Pressure cook the black chickpeas with enough water and a pinch of salt until they are soft and cooked through (about 4-5 whistles). Alternatively, you can cook them in a pot until tender.
Heat oil in a pan or pot over medium heat. Add the finely chopped onions and sauté until they turn light golden brown.
Add the ginger-garlic paste and sauté for about a minute until fragrant.
Add the finely chopped tomatoes and cook until they soften and the oil starts to separate from the sides.
Add turmeric powder, red chili powder, coriander powder, and cumin powder. Sauté for a minute, stirring continuously.
Add the cooked black chickpeas along with some of the cooking liquid. Mix well.
Add salt to taste and garam masala. Bring the gravy to a simmer , then reduce the heat and let it simmer for 10-15 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together. Adjust the consistency of the gravy by adding more cooking liquid or water if needed.
Garnish with freshly chopped coriander leaves.
Serve the kala chana hot with rice, roti, naan, or as a side dish.
