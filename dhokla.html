Instructions

In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the besan, sour curd, ginger-green chili paste, turmeric powder, sugar, and salt. Add a little water at a time to form a smooth, medium-thick batter. Ensure there are no lumps.
Cover the batter and let it rest for 15-20 minutes.
Grease a shallow dish or a dhokla steamer plate with oil.
Just before steaming, add eno (fruit salt) to the batter. Gently mix it in until the batter becomes light and frothy. Do not overmix.
Pour the batter immediately into the greased dish or steamer plate.
Steam the dhokla in a steamer for 15-20 minutes on medium heat, or until a toothpick inserted in the center comes out clean.
Once cooked, let the dhokla cool slightly before cutting it into squares or diamond shapes.

For Tempering

Heat oil in a small pan over medium heat. Add mustard seeds and let them splutter.
Add sesame seeds and slit green chilies. Sauté for a few seconds.
Add water and sugar to the pan. Bring it to a boil and let the sugar dissolve. Switch off the heat.
Pour the tempering evenly over the cut dhokla pieces.
Garnish with fresh coriander leaves.
Serve the dhokla warm with green chutney or tamarind chutney.
