Instructions

Peel the onions and slice them into rings, about 1/4 inch thick. Separate the rings.
In a shallow dish, whisk together the flour, cornstarch, baking powder, salt, and black pepper.
In another shallow dish, pour the milk or buttermilk.
Heat vegetable oil in a deep frying pan over medium-high heat.
Dip each onion ring into the flour mixture, then into the milk/buttermilk, and then back into the flour mixture to ensure a good coating.
Carefully add the coated onion rings to the hot oil in batches, ensuring not to overcrowd the pan.
Fry the onion rings until they are golden brown and crispy, about 2-3 minutes per side.
Remove the fried onion rings with a slotted spoon and place them on a paper towel-lined plate to drain excess oil.
Serve immediately with your favorite dipping sauce, such as ranch, ketchup, or a spicy mayo.
