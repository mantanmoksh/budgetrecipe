Instructions

Soak Urad Dal: Rinse urad dal thoroughly. Soak in enough water for at least 4-5 hours, or overnight, until it doubles in volume.
Grind the Dal: Drain all the water from the soaked dal. Grind it in a blender or wet grinder with minimal water to a smooth, thick, and fluffy batter. The consistency should be such that you can shape the vadas.
Add Ingredients: Transfer the batter to a bowl. Add grated ginger, finely chopped green chilies, chopped curry leaves, and salt to taste. Mix well.
Heat Oil: Heat oil in a deep frying pan or kadai over medium heat. The oil should be hot enough for the vadas to float immediately upon dropping.
Shape the Vadas: Wet your hands. Take a small portion of the batter (about 2 tablespoons). Shape it into a ball and then flatten it slightly. Make a hole in the center with your thumb.
Fry the Vadas: Gently slide the shaped vada into the hot oil. Fry a few vadas at a time, ensuring not to overcrowd the pan.
Fry Until Golden: Fry the vadas on medium heat, flipping occasionally, until they turn golden brown and crispy on all sides. This will take about 4-5 minutes per batch.
Drain and Serve: Remove the fried vadas with a slotted spoon and place them on a paper towel to drain excess oil. Serve hot with sambar and coconut chutney.
