Mutton Pepper Fry

Instructions

Wash the mutton pieces thoroughly. In a bowl, add the mutton, turmeric powder, coriander powder, cumin powder, and salt. Mix well and set aside for at least 15-20 minutes.
Heat oil in a heavy-bottomed pan or pressure cooker over medium heat. Add the finely chopped onions and sauté until they turn light golden brown.
Add the ginger-garlic paste and curry leaves. Sauté for about a minute until fragrant.
Add the marinated mutton pieces to the pan and sauté for about 5-7 minutes until the color changes.
Add about 1/2 cup of water (or more if needed, depending on the tenderness of the mutton and your preferred consistency). Cover and cook on low heat until the mutton is tender. This might take around 30-40 minutes in a regular pan, or 4-5 whistles in a pressure cooker. Ensure the water has mostly dried up.
Once the mutton is tender and the water has reduced, add the freshly crushed black pepper. Mix well, ensuring the pepper coats the mutton pieces evenly.
Continue to cook on medium-low heat for another 5-7 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the mutton is well-coated with the pepper and the mixture becomes slightly dry.
Garnish with freshly chopped coriander leaves.
Serve the hot mutton pepper fry as a flavorful side dish with rice, roti, or naan.
