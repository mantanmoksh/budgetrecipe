Fried Fish

Crispy and flavorful fish, coated in a spiced batter and deep-fried to golden perfection.

Instructions

Rinse the fish fillet under cold water and pat it completely dry with paper towels. Cut it into desired pieces if it's a large fillet.
In a bowl, combine gram flour, rice flour, ginger-garlic paste, red chili powder, turmeric powder, coriander powder, garam masala, lemon juice, and salt.
Gradually add a little water to the dry ingredients and mix well to form a smooth and medium-thick batter. Ensure there are no lumps. The batter should coat the fish pieces evenly.
Heat vegetable oil in a deep pan or kadai over medium-high heat. The oil should be hot enough for frying but not smoking.
Dip each fish piece into the prepared batter, ensuring it's fully coated.
Carefully slide the coated fish pieces into the hot oil, one at a time, ensuring not to overcrowd the pan. Fry in batches if necessary.
Fry the fish for about 3-5 minutes on each side, or until it turns golden brown and crispy. Ensure the fish is cooked through and flakes easily with a fork.
Remove the fried fish from the oil using a slotted spoon or tongs and place them on a paper towel-lined plate to drain excess oil.
Serve the hot fish fry immediately with lemon wedges, chaat masala (optional), and your favorite chutney or sauce.
