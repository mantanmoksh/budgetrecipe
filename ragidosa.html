Instructions

Combine Dry Ingredients: In a large bowl, mix together the ragi flour, rice flour (if using), urad dal flour (if using), cumin seeds, and salt.
Add Water Gradually: Gradually add water to the dry ingredients, mixing continuously with a whisk or your hand to avoid lumps. The batter should be smooth and have a thin, flowing consistency, similar to regular dosa batter.
Rest the Batter: Cover the bowl and let the batter rest for at least 30 minutes. This allows the flours to hydrate well. For softer dosas, you can ferment the batter for 2-3 hours or even overnight in a warm place.
Heat the Tawa: Heat a flat griddle or tawa (preferably non-stick) over medium-high heat. Once the tawa is hot, lightly grease it with oil or ghee.
Pour the Batter: Pour a ladleful of the batter in the center of the hot tawa. Using the back of the ladle, gently spread the batter in a circular motion to form a thin dosa.
Cook the Dosa: Drizzle a little oil or ghee around the edges and on the surface of the dosa. Cook on medium heat until the bottom turns golden brown and the edges start to lift.
Flip and Cook: Gently flip the dosa with a spatula and cook the other side for a minute or two until it is lightly golden.
Serve Hot: Fold the ragi dosa in half or roll it up and serve hot with sambar, chutney, or podi (lentil powder). Repeat with the remaining batter.
