Instructions

Wash the basmati rice thoroughly and soak it in water for at least 30 minutes.
Marinate the chicken pieces with yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, turmeric powder, red chili powder, biryani masala, garam masala, green chilies, mint leaves, coriander leaves, and salt. Mix well and let it marinate for at least 1 hour (or longer in the refrigerator).
In a heavy-bottomed pot or Dutch oven, heat oil or ghee. Add a few whole spices (like bay leaf, cardamom, cloves, cinnamon stick - optional) and saut√© for a few seconds.
Layer half of the soaked rice at the bottom of the pot. Spread half of the marinated chicken over the rice.
Sprinkle some fried onions (birista), chopped mint, and coriander leaves over the chicken.
Layer the remaining rice over the chicken. Pour the saffron milk over the rice. Top with the remaining fried onions, mint, and coriander leaves.
Cover the pot tightly with a lid. Cook on medium-low heat for 15-20 minutes, or until the rice is cooked and the chicken is tender. For 'dum' cooking, you can place a tawa (griddle) under the pot to prevent direct heat and cook on low heat for about 25-30 minutes.
Once cooked, let the biryani rest for 10-15 minutes before opening the lid. This allows the flavors to meld together.
Gently fluff the biryani with a fork before serving. Serve hot with raita (yogurt dip) and mirchi ka salan (chili curry).

Optional Vegetarian Variation: Replace chicken with mixed vegetables like carrots, peas, potatoes, and cauliflower. You can also use paneer (Indian cheese).
