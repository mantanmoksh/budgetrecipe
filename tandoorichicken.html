Tandoori Chicken

Classic Indian chicken marinated in yogurt and spices, traditionally cooked in a tandoor.

Instructions

Make deep slits on the chicken leg piece. This helps the marinade penetrate deeply.
Rub the chicken with lemon juice and 1/2 tsp of salt. Let it rest for 15-20 minutes. This helps tenderize the chicken.
In a separate bowl, whisk together the thick yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, tandoori masala, red chili powder, turmeric powder, cumin powder, garam masala, mustard oil (or vegetable oil), and salt to taste.
Apply this second marinade generously all over the chicken, ensuring the marinade gets into the slits.
Cover the marinated chicken and refrigerate for at least 2 hours. For a more intense flavor, marinate overnight.
Grilling: Preheat your grill to medium-high heat. Place the marinated chicken on the grill and cook for about 20-25 minutes, turning occasionally, until it's cooked through and has a nice char.
Baking: Preheat your oven to 200°C (400°F). Place the marinated chicken on a baking tray lined with parchment paper. Bake for 25-30 minutes, or until cooked through and slightly charred. You can broil for the last few minutes for extra color.
Tandoor (Traditional): Preheat your tandoor to the appropriate temperature. Thread the marinated chicken onto skewers and cook in the tandoor until cooked through and charred. This method will yield the most authentic flavor.
Once cooked, remove the chicken from the grill, oven, or tandoor.
You can brush the cooked chicken with a little melted butter for extra richness (optional).
Serve hot with lemon wedges, sliced onions, and mint chutney.
