Instructions

Wash the eggplants and make two slits in each, forming an 'X' shape, without cutting through the stem.
**For the stuffing:** In a small bowl, mix together roasted peanut powder, turmeric powder, red chili powder, coriander powder, cumin powder, tamarind pulp, and salt. Add a little water if needed to make a thick paste.
Stuff each eggplant with the prepared stuffing mixture, gently pushing it into the slits.
Heat oil in a pot or pan over medium heat. Add mustard seeds and fenugreek seeds. Let them splutter.
Add finely chopped onions and sauté until golden brown.
Add ginger-garlic paste and sauté for a minute until fragrant.
Stir in finely chopped tomatoes and cook until they soften and the oil starts to separate.
Gently place the stuffed eggplants in the pot. Add any leftover stuffing mixture.
Add about 1/2 cup of water (or more, depending on the desired gravy consistency). Bring to a simmer, cover the pot, and cook on low heat for 15-20 minutes, or until the eggplants are tender.
Stir occasionally to prevent sticking. If the gravy is too thick, add a little more water. Adjust salt to taste.
Garnish with fresh coriander leaves (optional) and serve hot with rice or roti.
