Kadai Paneer

A semi-dry, spicy, and flavorful dish with cottage cheese (paneer) cooked in a kadai (wok) with onions, bell peppers, and freshly ground spices.

Instructions

For Kadai Masala: Dry roast coriander seeds, cumin seeds, kasuri methi, and dry red chilies in a pan over low heat until fragrant. Let them cool and then grind them into a coarse powder. Keep aside.
Heat oil in a kadai (wok) or a heavy-bottomed pan over medium heat. Add the roughly chopped onions and sauté until they turn light golden brown.
Add the ginger-garlic paste and sauté for about a minute until fragrant.
Add the roughly chopped tomatoes and cook until they soften and the oil starts to separate from the sides.
Add turmeric powder, red chili powder, and coriander powder. Sauté for a minute, stirring continuously.
Add the cubed bell peppers and sauté for 2-3 minutes until they are slightly tender but still have a bite.
Add the prepared kadai masala and salt to taste. Mix well and cook for another 2-3 minutes.
Gently add the paneer cubes to the kadai. Mix carefully to coat them with the masala. Cook for 2-3 minutes, stirring gently to avoid breaking the paneer.
Crush the extra kasuri methi with your palms and add it to the kadai. Stir well.
Sprinkle garam masala and garnish with freshly chopped coriander leaves.
Serve the kadai paneer hot with roti, naan, paratha, or rice.
