Instructions

Prepare the Potato Mixture (Vada): Heat 1 tbsp of oil in a pan. Add mustard seeds and let them splutter. Add asafoetida and curry leaves, sauté for a few seconds.
Add ginger-garlic-green chili paste and sauté for a minute until fragrant.
Add turmeric powder and mashed potatoes. Mix well. Add chopped coriander leaves and salt to taste. Mix everything thoroughly and cook for 2-3 minutes.
Remove the potato mixture from the heat and let it cool slightly. Divide the mixture into equal-sized balls (vadas).
Prepare the Batter: In a bowl, mix gram flour, turmeric powder, baking soda, and salt. Gradually add water and whisk to form a smooth, thick batter (it should coat the back of a spoon).
Fry the Vadas: Heat oil in a deep pan or kadhai over medium heat.
Dip each potato ball into the gram flour batter, ensuring it's evenly coated.
Gently drop the coated potato balls into the hot oil. Fry them in batches, ensuring not to overcrowd the pan.
Fry the vadas until they are golden brown and crispy on all sides. This will take about 3-4 minutes per batch.
Remove the fried vadas with a slotted spoon and place them on a paper towel-lined plate to drain excess oil.
Assemble the Vada Pav: Slit the pav (burger buns) horizontally, without separating the two halves completely.
Spread garlic chutney on one side of the slit pav and green chutney on the other side.
Place a hot vada in between the two sides of the pav.
Serve the vada pav hot. You can also serve it with fried green chilies for an extra spicy kick.
