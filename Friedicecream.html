Instructions

Scoop the ice cream into firm balls (about 1/2 cup each) and place them on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper. Freeze for at least 2 hours, or until very firm.
In a shallow dish, combine the crushed cornflakes and shredded coconut. In another shallow dish, place the flour. In a third shallow dish, beat the egg.
Working quickly, roll each frozen ice cream ball in the flour, then dip it in the beaten egg, and finally coat it thoroughly with the cornflake-coconut mixture. Make sure the coating is even and covers the entire ball.
Return the coated ice cream balls to the lined baking sheet and freeze again for at least 1 hour to help them hold their shape during frying.
Heat about 2-3 inches of vegetable oil in a deep pot or Dutch oven to 375¬∞F (190¬∞C). Use a thermometer to ensure the oil is at the correct temperature.
Carefully drop one or two ice cream balls at a time into the hot oil. Fry for only 10-15 seconds per side, until the coating isgolden brown and crispy. Do not overcook, or the ice cream will melt.
Remove the fried ice cream balls with a slotted spoon and place them on a plate lined with paper towels to drain any excess oil.
Serve immediately. Drizzle with honey or chocolate syrup, if desired. You can also garnish with whipped cream, cinnamon, or a sprinkle of nuts.
